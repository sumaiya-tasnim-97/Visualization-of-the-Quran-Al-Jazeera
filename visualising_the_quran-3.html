01 — The Quran at a Glance

Structure & Revelation

The Quran was revealed in Arabic to the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ through the angel Gabriel over a period of 23 years — beginning in 609 CE and concluding at the Prophet's death in 632 CE. It is divided into Meccan surahs (revealed before the Hijra in 622 CE) and Medinan surahs (revealed after).

30 Sections جُزْء (Juz')
114 Chapters سُورَة (Surahs)
6,236 Verses آيَة (Ayat)
77,432 Words كَلِمَة
23 Years of Revelation 609–632 CE
✓ Cross-Check
The 30 sections (Juz'), 114 chapters, and 23-year revelation period are universally agreed upon. The verse count of 6,236 is the most commonly cited figure (some counts range 6,214–6,236 depending on inclusion of the Bismillah; 6,236 refers to the standard Hafs recitation). The word count of 77,432 aligns with scholarly references to the Al-Madina Al-Munawwara Quran (Arabic Edition).
Surah Al-'Alaq (96) : 1 — First Verse Revealed
ٱقْرَأْ بِٱسْمِ رَبِّكَ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَ
"Read, in the Name of your Lord Who created…"
This is the first verse ever revealed to the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ in the Cave of Hira, marking the commencement of the 23-year revelation. The highlighted word ٱقْرَأْ (Iqra — "Read/Recite") is the Quran's very first command to humanity — emphasising the centrality of knowledge and recitation that the Al Jazeera infographic references.

02 — The Quran & Ramadan

Revelation in the Holy Month

The Quran was first revealed during Ramadan, the ninth month of the Islamic lunar calendar — the month of fasting and reflection. Ramadan lasts either 29 or 30 days, depending on the sighting of the moon. At its conclusion, Muslims celebrate Eid al-Fitr, the festival of breaking the fast.

Surah Al-Baqarah (2) : 185 — Ramadan as Month of Quran
شَهْرُ رَمَضَانَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أُنزِلَ فِيهِ ٱلْقُرْءَانُ هُدًۭى لِّلنَّاسِ وَبَيِّنَـٰتٍۢ مِّنَ ٱلْهُدَىٰ وَٱلْفُرْقَانِ ۚ فَمَن شَهِدَ مِنكُمُ ٱلشَّهْرَ فَلْيَصُمْهُ
"The month of Ramadan is the one in which the Quran was revealed, a guidance for people, and clear proofs of guidance and a criterion. So whoever among you witnesses this month, let them fast…"
This verse is the primary Quranic proof for the claim in the infographic that "the Quran was first revealed during the month of Ramadan." The highlighted phrase شَهْرُ رَمَضَانَ ٱلَّذِىٓ أُنزِلَ فِيهِ ٱلْقُرْءَانُ literally translates to "the month of Ramadan in which the Quran was sent down." It also commands fasting (فَلْيَصُمْهُ) for those who witness the month, confirming the infographic's description of Sawm.
Surah Al-Baqarah (2) : 183 — Fasting is Prescribed
يَـٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُوا۟ كُتِبَ عَلَيْكُمُ ٱلصِّيَامُ كَمَا كُتِبَ عَلَى ٱلَّذِينَ مِن قَبْلِكُمْ لَعَلَّكُمْ تَتَّقُونَ
"O believers! Fasting is prescribed for you — as it was for those before you — so perhaps you will become mindful of Allah."
The infographic describes Sawm (fasting) as abstinence from eating, drinking, smoking and sexual relations "to achieve greater taqwa, or consciousness of God." This verse is the exact Quranic source for that claim — the phrase لَعَلَّكُمْ تَتَّقُونَ (so that you may attain taqwa) confirms the purpose stated in the infographic.
Surah Al-Qadr (97) : 1–5 — The Night of Power
إِنَّآ أَنزَلْنَـٰهُ فِى لَيْلَةِ ٱلْقَدْرِ ١ وَمَآ أَدْرَىٰكَ مَا لَيْلَةُ ٱلْقَدْرِ ٢ لَيْلَةُ ٱلْقَدْرِ خَيْرٌۭ مِّنْ أَلْفِ شَهْرٍۢ ٣ تَنَزَّلُ ٱلْمَلَـٰٓئِكَةُ وَٱلرُّوحُ فِيهَا بِإِذْنِ رَبِّهِم مِّن كُلِّ أَمْرٍۢ ٤ سَلَـٰمٌ هِىَ حَتَّىٰ مَطْلَعِ ٱلْفَجْرِ ٥
"Indeed, We sent it (the Quran) down on the Night of Decree. ① And what can make you know what is the Night of Decree? ② The Night of Decree is better than a thousand months. ③ The angels descend therein, and the Spirit, by permission of their Lord, for every matter. ④ It is all peace until the break of dawn. ⑤"
The entire Surah Al-Qadr (Chapter 97) is dedicated to Laylat al-Qadr. The infographic states "praying on that night is believed to be better than a thousand months of worship" — this is confirmed by verse 3: لَيْلَةُ ٱلْقَدْرِ خَيْرٌۭ مِّنْ أَلْفِ شَهْرٍۢ. The descending of angels mentioned in the infographic is confirmed by verse 4: تَنَزَّلُ ٱلْمَلَـٰٓئِكَةُ. The Cave of Hira is in Mecca; the first verses revealed there (Al-'Alaq 96:1) occurred during this blessed night.
✓ Cross-Check — Laylat al-Qadr Date
The infographic correctly states Laylat al-Qadr falls "on one of the odd-numbered nights during the final 10 days of Ramadan." This is confirmed by multiple authentic hadiths in Sahih Al-Bukhari and Sahih Muslim, which record that the Prophet ﷺ instructed: "Seek it in the last ten nights of Ramadan on the odd nights." (Al-Bukhari, Vol.3, Book 32, No. 234)

⚠ Note on Cross-Check — Word Counts Include Derivatives
The infographic notes with an asterisk (*) that counts for Angels, Devils, Guidance, Mercy, Calamity, Thanksgiving, The Mind, and The Light include the derivatives of these words. Scholars have varying counts depending on the methodology (root word only vs. all derivatives, all grammatical forms). The figures cited align with those published by Al-Azhar scholars and widely reproduced in Islamic education materials, including the source referenced by Al Jazeera: Al-Madina Al-Munawwara Quran (Arabic Edition). Life/Death at 145 each and Man/Woman at 24 each are among the most consistently verified across independent sources.

The 145 count for both life (ٱلْحَيَوٰة) and death (ٱلْمَوْت) uses a broad methodology that includes noun forms, verb conjugations, and all derivatives from each root (ح-ي-ي for life; م-و-ت for death). Surah An-Nisa (4) : 1 — Man & Woman Created as One
يَـٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلنَّاسُ ٱتَّقُوا۟ رَبَّكُمُ ٱلَّذِى خَلَقَكُم مِّن نَّفْسٍۢ وَٰحِدَةٍۢ وَخَلَقَ مِنْهَا زَوْجَهَا وَبَثَّ مِنْهُمَا رِجَالًۭا كَثِيرًۭا وَنِسَآءًۭ
"O humanity! Be mindful of your Lord Who created you from a single soul, and from it created its mate, and through them spread countless men and women."
This verse is one of the foundational Quranic references to both رِجَالًۭا (men/rijāl) and نِسَآءًۭ (women/nisā') together. Surah An-Nisa (Chapter 4), whose very name means "The Women," contains the highest concentration of the word نِسَآء. The symmetry of man appearing 24 times and woman appearing 24 times — despite each word having independent grammatical occurrences — is one of the most cited numerical balances in Quranic studies.
Surah Al-Ahzab (33) : 35 — Men and Women Named Together Equally
إِنَّ ٱلْمُسْلِمِينَ وَٱلْمُسْلِمَـٰتِ وَٱلْمُؤْمِنِينَ وَٱلْمُؤْمِنَـٰتِ وَٱلْقَـٰنِتِينَ وَٱلْقَـٰنِتَـٰتِ وَٱلصَّـٰدِقِينَ وَٱلصَّـٰدِقَـٰتِ وَٱلصَّـٰبِرِينَ وَٱلصَّـٰبِرَٰتِ وَٱلْخَـٰشِعِينَ وَٱلْخَـٰشِعَـٰتِ وَٱلْمُتَصَدِّقِينَ وَٱلْمُتَصَدِّقَـٰتِ وَٱلصَّـٰٓئِمِينَ وَٱلصَّـٰٓئِمَـٰتِ وَٱلْحَـٰفِظِينَ فُرُوجَهُمْ وَٱلْحَـٰفِظَـٰتِ وَٱلذَّٰكِرِينَ ٱللَّهَ كَثِيرًۭا وَٱلذَّٰكِرَٰتِ أَعَدَّ ٱللَّهُ لَهُم مَّغْفِرَةًۭ وَأَجْرًا عَظِيمًۭا
"Indeed, Muslim men and Muslim women, believing men and believing women, devout men and devout women, truthful men and truthful women, patient men and patient women, humble men and humble women, charitable men and charitable women, fasting men and fasting women, men who guard their chastity and women who do so, and men who remember Allah often and women who do so — for all of them Allah has prepared forgiveness and a great reward."
This verse is perhaps the Quran's most striking example of the deliberate pairing of men and women as equal moral agents. Every quality is listed twice — once for men (masculine form) and once for women (feminine form) — 10 qualities, 20 parallel phrases. The phrasing ٱلْمُسْلِمِينَ وَٱلْمُسْلِمَـٰتِ (Muslim men and Muslim women) establishes the pattern that runs throughout. This structural equality mirrors the statistical symmetry of رَجُل (man) = 24 and مَرْأَة / ٱمْرَأَة (woman) = 24 across the Quran's text.
Surah Al-Hujurat (49) : 13 — Humanity Created from Man and Woman
يَـٰٓأَيُّهَا ٱلنَّاسُ إِنَّا خَلَقْنَـٰكُم مِّن ذَكَرٍۢ وَأُنثَىٰ وَجَعَلْنَـٰكُمْ شُعُوبًۭا وَقَبَآئِلَ لِتَعَارَفُوٓا۟ ۚ إِنَّ أَكْرَمَكُمْ عِندَ ٱللَّهِ أَتْقَىٰكُمْ
"O humanity! Indeed We created you from a male and a female, and made you into peoples and tribes so that you may get to know one another. Surely the most noble of you in the sight of Allah is the most righteous (God-conscious) among you."
Here ذَكَرٍۢ (male/man) and أُنثَىٰ (female/woman) appear side by side as the two equal foundations of all humanity. This verse is frequently cited in the context of gender equality in the Quran — the only distinction drawn between people is taqwa (God-consciousness), not gender. The pairing of ذَكَر and أُنثَى in the Quran mirrors the 24–24 symmetry of رَجُل and مَرْأَة cited in the infographic.

⚠ Important Nuance — Muhammad's Mentions
The infographic states "The Prophet Muhammad is mentioned in the most number of chapters in the Quran, appearing 47 times." This requires clarification: by name, Muhammad ﷺ is mentioned only 4 times directly (in 3:144, 33:40, 47:2, 48:29). However, he is the subject of 47 chapters (surahs) and addressed indirectly as "the Prophet" or "the Messenger" hundreds of times. The figure of 47 thus refers to the number of surahs in which he appears — not direct name mentions. The bar chart above correctly shows 4 direct name mentions, while the 47-surah figure is a separate measure confirmed in the Al Jazeera infographic's own visual data.

إِنَّا نَحْنُ نَزَّلْنَا ٱلذِّكْرَ وَإِنَّا لَهُۥ لَحَـٰفِظُونَ
